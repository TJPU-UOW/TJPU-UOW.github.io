Teachers

Zhitao Xiao

Prof. Zhitao Xiao is a full professor in the School of Electronics and Information
Engineering at Tianjin Polytech University, and directs the Tianjin Key Laboratory
of Photoelectric Detection Technology and System. He is elected fellow of Tianjin
Society of Image and Graphics. He is enrolled the first class of Tianjin ‘131’
Innovative Talent Cultivate Project. His current research interests include robot
vision, intelligent signal processing and pattern recognition.

Philip Olurotimi Ogunbona

Prof. Philip O. Ogunbona obtained the DIC and PhD for research conducted in the field of Image Processing at the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Imperial College of Science, Medicine and Technology, University of London. He joined the University of Wollongong, School of Electrical in 1990 and left to join the Visual Information Processing Lab, Motorola Labs in 8 years later. He was Principal Research Engineer and later became the foundation Manager of the Digital Media Collection and Management Lab, Motorola Labs, Sydney. He was part of the Motorola team that worked on the JPEG2000 standardization. In 2004, he returned to the University of Wollongong and became a full professor and head of School. He directs of the Centre for Visual Information Processing and Content Management Research. He currently has four patents filed in the US and has published over 100 journal and conference papers. He is a Senior Member of the IEEE and member of the IEEE NSW Committee. His current research interests include image and video processing, video surveillance, multimedia security and multimedia content management.

Guangxu Li

Dr. Guangxu Li received his B.E. and M.E. degrees in automation engineering
from Liaoning University of Technology in 2006 and 2009, the Ph.D. degree
from Kyushu Institute of Technology in 2013, respectively. He is a lecturer
in the school of electronics and information engineering at Tianjin Polyte-
-chnic University. His research interests are focused on medical image pro-
-cessing and image-guided surgical system.
